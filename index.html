Identifying the Network

I was tasked with creating a single page website displaying a collection of categorised material related to internet infrastructure around Wellington. On this page I have curated an array of images I believe to be relevant to internet infrastructure. In addition to indoor and outdoor evidence, I have also chosen to include companies and locations linked to internet activity.

ISPs (Internet Service Providers)

Internet Service Providers connect organizations, businesses and individuals with the internet. ISPs maintain servers and provide routers and modems to individuals.

Spark NZ

Spark is one of the more popular ISPs in New Zealand. There are many Spark locations around Wellington including their business hub at 168 Taranaki Street (pictured).

2Degrees

2Degrees is another popular New Zealand telecommunications company. They also have multiple locations around Wellington including this one at 71 Cuba St.

One NZ

One NZ is Vodafone NZ's new name. This one is located at 90 Cuba Street.

Cameras

Some cameras connect to the internet in order to function properly. Often local network connectivity is required to transmit the video images to a monitoring facility or storage base.

Speed Cameras

Traffic enforcement cameras such as this one on Karo Road, are used to enforce speed limits around Wellington. Internet connectivity is likely required to transmit the captured data to a database or server in order for it to be analysed.

Public Security Cameras

These cameras are used to monitor the behaviour in more crowded areas of Wellington - such as Courtenay Place where this one is located. Similar to the speed camera, the footage captured on this is likely transmited using internet connectivity to a database, server or monitoring facility.

Private Cameras

This camera is in place for general security monitoring. It is likely linked (using internet connection) to a broader network that encompasses the building's entire security system.

Internal + External Building Infrastructure

Below are a collection of items located in and outside people's apartments that allow for an internet connection.

Router

Routers are used for a multitude of internet related activites. Most notably they allow users to connect their devices on a network. Routers also connect this network with multiple others - creating a line of communication between devices. Routers are able to receive data packets from user's devices and idenitfy the most efficient pathway of 'delivering' these.

ONT (Optical Network Terminal)

This ONT, located inside an apartment, connects the router to the broadband provider. In this case it connects the router to Chorus' fibre network.

Manholes

Manholes allow infrastructure-providing-companies like Chorus to access the underground network of cables that are responsible for transmitting broadband/phone services around Wellington.

Various Connectors

Here are various cables and wallboxes as evidence of Chorus' infrastructure. These are placed inside and outside Wellington's buildings. They would likely be responsible for transmitting data as well as connecting networks within buildings and around the city.

Data Centres

Data Centres are facilities that house servers and computing equipment. DCs can store and process large amounts of data and provide a location to host online services such as websites. These were a bit harder to find around Wellington, but they do exist. The facilities are only accessible through guided tours, but I was able to photograph the buildings they are located in.

Datacom

Datacom on Abel Smith Street is one of the easier-to-find data centres in Wellington.

Spark DC

One of Spark's 12 data centres is located on Featherston Street.

Chorus DC

On Level 10 of the Aon Centre at 1 Willis Street is one of Chorus' data centres.

Devices

Our devices are usually the way we connect to the internet. They are able to exchange data with other devices because of their connectivity. I've collected some examples of devices I use to connect to the internet on a daily basis.

TVs

TVs can often connect to your at home Wi-Fi with the network's password. Some are also able to connect directly via ethernet cable. Smart TVs with built in systems such as Android TV also have access to internet browsers.

Smart Phone

The majority of modern smart phones are able to access the internet via Wi-Fi or via cellular networks e.g. 5G. In addition to this, they are able to act as mobile hotspots, allowing other devices to connect to the internet by sharing their cellular internet connection.

Echo Dot - Alexa

Echo Dots are connected to the internet via Wi-Fi network. The internet connection has to be relied on in order for the device to work e.g. internet is needed to access updated weather information.

Ipad

Ipads have built in ability to connect to the internet via Wi-Fi. Some models also have a SIM card slot allowing for cellular data to be used as well.
